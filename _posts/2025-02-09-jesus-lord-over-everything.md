_Matthew 21:1-11_

#### Discussion Questions

- Think about a time someone sacrificed something valuable to help you. How did that impact you? What did it reveal about their priorities?
- In Matthew 21:1-3, Jesus tells his disciples to get a donkey, saying simply, "The Master needs it." What does this reveal about Jesus' authority? How do you respond when Jesus asks you to do something that seems unclear or uncomfortable?
- The crowd gave their most valuable possessions (cloaks) and limited resources (palm branches) to honor Jesus. What prevents us from offering our best to Jesus today? What would it look like to offer our "cloaks and branches"?
- Looking at verses 10-11, the city was "shaken" by Jesus' arrival but had different responses. Which of the three groups in Matthew (the crowd, religious leaders, or disciples) do you most relate to in your current relationship with Jesus? Why?

#### Weekly Challenge

Choose one:
- Take time each morning this week to declare "Jesus is Lord" over specific areas of your life—your time, resources, relationships, work.
- Look for one practical way to serve others in your community this week, viewing it as service to Jesus.
- Open your home to host or attend a small group, stepping out of your comfort zone to build biblical community.
